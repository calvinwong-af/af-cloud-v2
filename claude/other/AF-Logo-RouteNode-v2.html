Hexagon rationale
Pointy-top hexagon places a vertex directly above the hub, giving the mark a natural upward orientation. The top node reads as the origin or inbound route; the two lower nodes as outbound destinations — mirroring how a freight hub actually works.
Three routes, not six
Using only 3 of the 6 possible vertices keeps the mark asymmetric and readable. A full 6-spoke wheel looks like a snowflake at small sizes — 3 routes are bold enough to survive at 16px.
Hub ring design
A white outer ring with a sky-blue filled centre creates a clear "node" metaphor. The ring also adds separation from the route lines at all sizes, preventing the centre from becoming a blob.

How integrity is maintained
The mark uses three distinct stroke-weight tiers. At 64–40px, detail is full. At 28px, stroke widths increase proportionally so lines don't disappear. At 20px and below, the endpoint dots and hub are oversized relative to the SVG viewBox — deliberately so, to ensure they punch through at screen resolution.
The 16px rule
16px is the hard minimum. Below this, the hub and route nodes merge into an unreadable blob regardless of stroke weight. In practice the mark is never used below 20px in UI — the 16px version exists only as a last-resort favicon fallback.
